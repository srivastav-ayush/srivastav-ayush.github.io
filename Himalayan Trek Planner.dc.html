The Himalayan Trek Calendar: Which Trek, Which Month, Which Year

Four quarters, 65 treks, one decision framework — which trek to do, in which month, and in what order across a multi-year progression.

By Ayush·11 min read·May 2026

Across the full April 2026 → March 2027 trekking year, 12+ treks score above 8.0 on a composite framework — distributed across all four quarters. A year of intentional trekking should include: one spring temple trek (Q1), one monsoon crossover (Q2), one Nepal/Sikkim autumn trek (Q3), and one winter snow summit (Q4). For a first-timer, the correct progression is Q1 Chopta (4,000m debut) → Q4 Kedarkantha (3,800m winter summit) → Q2 Kashmir Great Lakes (4,000m monsoon) → Q3 Nepal, in that order.

How the composite score is calculated

Year 1: Chopta (April) + Kedarkantha (December). Year 2: Kashmir Great Lakes + Annapurna Base Camp. Year 3: Chadar + Everest Base Camp. Each step adds ~500m of altitude and a new terrain type.

Data sources: Trek the Himalayas, Indiahikes, Nepal Mountain Trekkers, Indiahikes seasonal reports, Nepal trekking permit databases. Scores reflect seasonal conditions only, not a trek's absolute ceiling — the same trek scores differently in different quarters.
